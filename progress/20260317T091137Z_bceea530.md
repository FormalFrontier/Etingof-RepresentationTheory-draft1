## Accomplished

Formalized the statement of Gabriel's theorem (Theorem 2.1.2) — a landmark classification
result stating that a connected quiver has finite representation type iff its underlying
undirected graph is a Dynkin diagram (A_n, D_n, E_6, E_7, E_8).

Introduced five supporting definitions to make the statement concrete:
- `QuiverRepresentationEquiv`: isomorphism of quiver representations (vertex-wise linear equivs commuting with arrow maps)
- `QuiverRepresentation.IsNonzero`: at least one vertex space is nontrivial
- `QuiverRepresentation.IsIndecomposable`: nonzero + no nontrivial vertex-wise complementary subrepresentation decomposition
- `HasFiniteRepresentationType`: finitely many iso classes of f.d. indecomposable representations
- `quiverUndirectedAdj` / `QuiverUndirectedConnected`: underlying undirected adjacency matrix and connectivity

All universes pinned to 0 via `FinQuiverRep` abbreviation for the quantification over
representations (needed to avoid universe polymorphism issues in the existential/universal).

## Accomplished

Proved the `hrank` lemma's i=j case in `exists_orthogonal_idempotents_for_simples`
(Theorem9_2_1.lean), eliminating the sorry at the diagonal idempotent rank computation.
The proof shows that E₁₁ in Wedderburn-Artin block σ(i) acts with rank 1 on simple
module M_i.

Proof strategy:
1. **Idempotency**: E₁₁² = E₁₁ implies a•(a•m) = a•m via `hsmul_eq`
2. **Nonzero image**: Two-sided ideal argument using ∑ E_{j0}·E₁₁·E_{0j} = I in Mat_d(k),
   lifted through WA.symm and Pi.single. If E₁₁ acts as zero, c_l acts as zero,
   contradicting `hc_identity`.
3. **Span argument**: Pick v₀ = a•m₀ ≠ 0 (fixed point of idempotent). A-simplicity of M_i
   gives m' = b•v₀ for any m'. Corner identity π(a*b*a) = c_val • π(a) (proved by
   working in the product ring via WA) shows a•m' ∈ span_k{v₀}.
4. **Rank**: smulRange = span_k{v₀}, then `finrank_span_singleton` gives rank = 1.

Also fixed several pre-existing compilation errors in Pi.single multiplication proofs,
hMatSimple, and hσ_inj.

### Key Finding: The current approach is fundamentally flawed

The existing code decomposes V×W into X-invariant complementary submodules M₁, M₂ (via `nilpotent_nontrivial_decomp` on X(v,w)=(Bw,Av)), then projects to V and W:
- pV = M₁.map fst, qV = M₂.map fst
- pW = M₁.map snd, qW = M₂.map snd

**IsCompl pV qV and IsCompl pW qW are unprovable** because projecting complementary submodules of V×W does NOT preserve complementarity. Counterexample: M₁ = span{(1,1)}, M₂ = span{(1,-1)} in ℂ×ℂ with X=0 gives pV = qV = ℂ.

### Three approaches analyzed, all require significant work

1. **Compatible chain basis (book's approach, Problem 6.9.1(c))**: Prove that cyclic generators in the PID decomposition of V×W as ℂ[X]-module can be chosen in V×{0} or {0}×W. With compatible generators, chains alternate V↔W components, making projections automatically complementary. Requires ~150 lines of ℂ[X]-module theory.

2. **BA-decomposition of V**: dim(ker BA) = dim(ker A) + dim(ker B) ≥ 2 (proved). Apply `nilpotent_nontrivial_decomp` to BA on V → V₁ ⊕ V₂. Then W₁ = A(V₁), W₂ = A(V₂). **Blocked**: ker A might not split along V₁⊕V₂, so W₁∩W₂ ≠ {0} and W₁+W₂ ≠ W in general.

3. **Q₂-endomorphism idempotent**: The X-commuting projection from V×W = M₁⊕M₂ is not block-diagonal (does not respect V⊕W grading), so it doesn't directly give a Q₂-endomorphism.

Implement the compatible chain basis approach (approach 1 above). This is the book's proof and the most mathematically natural route. Specifically:

1. Prove that for X(v,w) = (Bw,Av) nilpotent on V×W, the PID cyclic decomposition can be done with generators in V×{0} or {0}×W
2. Show that with compatible generators, grouping chains into two non-empty groups gives IsCompl for both V and W projections
3. Use this to fill the sorry in `q2_nontrivial_decomp`

Key mathematical insight for step 1: ker X = ker A × ker B decomposes naturally along V and W. For chains of length 1, generators are already in ker X and can be chosen in V×{0} or {0}×W. For chains of length ≥ 2, the generator can be replaced by (v,0) or (0,w) because X alternates the V and W components. The main subtlety is showing that the modified generators still form a valid PID decomposition.

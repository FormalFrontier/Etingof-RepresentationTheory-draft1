## Finding 7: `Decidable.casesOn` pattern is consistent (POSITIVE)

The `Decidable.casesOn` workaround pattern for avoiding instance diamonds is used consistently across Definition6_6_3, Definition6_6_4, and Proposition6_6_6:

1. Fix `inst v i` to `.isTrue`/`.isFalse` via `have h_di : inst i i = .isTrue rfl`
2. `revert` terms, `unfold` definitions, `rw [h_di]`, `intro` back
3. Both sides reduce definitionally → `rfl`

This pattern appears in `reflFunctorPlus_mapLinear_ne_ne`, `reflFunctorMinus_mapLinear_ne_ne`, `reflFunctorMinus_mapLinear_ne_eq`, `reflFunctorMinus_mkQ`, and `reversedArrow_*`. It's the canonical approach for this codebase.

**Recommendation for #1724 and #1726**: Follow this exact pattern. The pattern is documented in the comments of Definition6_6_4.lean (lines 91–106, 113).

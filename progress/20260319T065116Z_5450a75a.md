## Accomplished

Investigated the nilpotent case of Problem 6.9.1 (issue #1191) — the last sorry in Problem6_9_1.lean. This is the proof that for an indecomposable Q₂ representation with AB nilpotent, |dim V - dim W| ≤ 1.

**Analysis**: The proof requires showing that the nilpotent operator X(v,w) = (Bw,Av) on V × W admits a chain basis compatible with the V ⊕ W decomposition. For indecomposable reps, this forces a single chain, giving |dim V - dim W| ≤ 1. This is Problem 6.9.1(c) in the book.

The difficulty is that:
1. A ℂ[X]-module decomposition of V × W does NOT directly give a Q₂ decomposition (the projections don't preserve disjointness).
2. The compatible chain basis requires either Jordan normal form theory for graded nilpotent operators, or a direct construction that shows the chains can be chosen to alternate between V and W.
3. Neither approach has readily available Mathlib infrastructure.

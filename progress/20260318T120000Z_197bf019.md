## Accomplished

Decomposed the proof of Corollary 6.8.3 (dimension vectors determine indecomposable representations) into structured helper lemmas with clear sorry boundaries.

**Proof structure built:**
1. `simpleAt_iso`: Simple representations at the same vertex are isomorphic. Proved 3 of 4 naturality cases; the self-loop case (a=p, b=p) is sorry'd but vacuous for Dynkin quivers (no self-loops).
2. `indecomposable_simpleRoot_isSimpleAt`: Trivially true from the definition of IsSimpleAt.
3. `reflectionFunctors_reduce_and_recover` (sorry'd): The main infrastructure gap — iterated reflection functors reduce indecomposable reps to simple ones following Theorem 6.8.1's reflection sequence.

**Main theorem proof:** Extracts the dimension vector as integer-valued function, proves it's non-negative and nonzero (from indecomposability), invokes Theorem 6.8.1 to get the reflection sequence, and delegates to the recovery lemma. One additional sorry for `hd_root` (showing dim vectors of indecomposables satisfy B(d,d)=2).

**Key finding:** The formalization has a structural gap between integer-level dimension vector reflections (Theorem 6.8.1) and representation-level reflection functors. Bridging this requires:
- Showing reflection functors change dimension vectors by simple reflections
- Iterated reflection functor application (each step changes the quiver)
- Preserving indecomposability through the functor chain
- Connecting the quiver Q to the adjacency matrix adj (currently disconnected in the theorem statement)

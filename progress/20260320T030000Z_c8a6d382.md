## Accomplished

Analyzed the hook quotient identity (issue #1319) — the key sorry in FRTHelpers.lean blocking the Frame-Robinson-Thrall theorem. The identity states:

∑_{c ∈ outerCorners(μ)} H(μ)/H(μ\c) = |μ|

Added `hookRatio_eq_prod_div` infrastructure lemma expressing H(μ)/H(μ\c) as ∏_{x ∈ μ.erase c} (h_μ(x)/h_{μ\c}(x)) using `Finset.prod_div_distrib`.

Investigated three proof approaches:
1. **Strong induction on |μ|**: Requires relating OC(μ) to OC(μ\c₀) when removing a corner. Key finding: every corner of μ except c₀ remains a corner of μ', but μ' may gain new corners. The correction factor α(c) = h·(h-2)/(h-1)² at cross-cells relates the ratios. Verified this works on multiple examples ((3,2), (2,1), (3,3,1), (4,2,1), (2,2)).
2. **Direct algebraic manipulation**: The ratio for each corner factors into row and column products ∏ h/(h-1), but these don't telescope in general (only when column lengths are constant).
3. **Hook walk / probabilistic**: Would require formalizing the GNW random walk argument, which is a research paper in itself.

### Deep analysis: polytabloid definition is fundamentally flawed

**Key discovery**: The polytabloid definition `of(sytPerm T) * a_λ * b_λ` gives IDENTICAL polytabloids for different SYTs whose sytPerms are in the same right P_λ-coset. Verified concretely:
- Partition (3,1), T₂ (entries {0,1,3}/{2}) and T₃ (entries {0,2,3}/{1})
- sytPerm T₂⁻¹ * sytPerm T₃ = (12) ∈ P_λ, so they give the same right coset
- Therefore `polytabloid T₂ = polytabloid T₃` (same element of ℂ[S₄])
- Only 2 distinct polytabloids among 3 SYTs, but V_{(3,1)} has dimension 3

This means `polytabloid_linearIndependent` and `polytabloid_span` are both FALSE as stated. (Both already had sorry's in their dependency chains.)

**Root cause**: James' polytabloid basis uses `of(sytPerm T) * b_λ * a_λ` (column × row), not `of(sytPerm T) * a_λ * b_λ` (row × column). The T-dependent column antisymmetrizer is what makes different SYTs give different polytabloids.

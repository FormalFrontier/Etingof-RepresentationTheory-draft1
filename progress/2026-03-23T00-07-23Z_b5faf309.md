## Next step

Prove `alternatingKostka_norm_sq_eq_one`. Two viable approaches:

**Approach A (bialternant formula):**
1. Prove `Δ · s_ν = sign(rev) · a_{ν+ρ}` where Δ is the file's Vandermonde, s_ν is the Schur polynomial, a_α is the alternant
2. Prove K_content(α, ν) = K(sort(α), ν) (Bender-Knuth invariance — SSYT counts depend only on content multiset)
3. Extract coefficients to get `alternatingKostkaInt(la, nu) = sign(rev) · δ_{la,nu}` directly
4. Norm-squared identity follows trivially: `∑_ν (sign(rev) · δ_{la,ν})² = 1`

**Approach B (character inner product):**
1. Use the already-proved expansion `θ_λ(σ) = ∑_ν L_{νλ} χ_ν(σ)` (from Young's Rule + coeff_eq_youngsRule_expansion)
2. Take norm-squared: `⟨θ_λ, θ_λ⟩ = ∑_ν L²_{νλ}` by `FDRep.char_orthonormal` (now usable since `spechtModuleFDRep_simple` is proved)
3. Prove Vandermonde coefficient orthogonality: `1/|S_n| ∑_σ θ_λ(σ)² = 1` from the polynomial definition

Both approaches require significant infrastructure. Approach A (bialternant) seems cleaner.

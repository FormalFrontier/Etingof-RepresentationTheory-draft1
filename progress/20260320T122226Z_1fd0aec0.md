### Key technical blocker
`Decidable.casesOn` is a recursor, not a match expression. The Lean kernel treats `inst i i` (a function application) as opaque in dependent positions. This prevents:
- `generalize` (result not type-correct when inst i i appears in multiple dependent positions)
- `rw` on hypotheses `hp : inst i i = .isTrue p` (motive not type-correct)
- `split` (doesn't recognize `Decidable.casesOn` as splittable)

The `cases inst i i` tactic works in isolated goals (like `ReversedAtVertexHom_at_first`) but fails when both `reversedArrow_eq_ne` and `ReversedAtVertexHom_at_first` are unfolded simultaneously.

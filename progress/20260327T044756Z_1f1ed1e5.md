## Accomplished

### Partially proved q2_nontrivial_decomp (issue #1691)

Decomposed the proof of `q2_nontrivial_decomp` in Problem6_9_1.lean using the off-diagonal operator X(v,w) = (Bw, Av) on V×W combined with `nilpotent_nontrivial_decomp`.

Proved 6 of 8 sub-goals:
1. **X is nilpotent**: Via X² = (BA, AB), BA nilpotent from AB nilpotent (shift lemma), X^(2(n+m)) = 0
2. **dim(ker X) ≥ 2**: ker X = ker A × ker B, with explicit LinearEquiv for finrank
3. **A maps pV→pW and qV→qW**: X-invariance of M₁, M₂ gives compatibility
4. **B maps pW→pV and qW→qV**: Same argument
5. **¬(pV=⊥ ∧ pW=⊥)**: If both projections are ⊥ then M₁=⊥, contradicting hM₁_ne
6. **¬(qV=⊥ ∧ qW=⊥)**: Same for M₂

## Current frontier

Two remaining sorrys in `q2_nontrivial_decomp`:
- `IsCompl pV qV` (pV = M₁.map fst, qV = M₂.map fst)
- `IsCompl pW qW` (pW = M₁.map snd, qW = M₂.map snd)

These require showing that projections of complementary X-invariant subspaces onto V and W give complementary subspaces. The fundamental difficulty: `Submodule.map` of complementary submodules through a non-injective map does not automatically give complements. A grading-compatible chain basis theory is needed.

### Proof strategy for (i) — irreducibility

The book's proof decomposes V|_A into character weight spaces (one per coset), uses simplicity of U on the stabilizer component, and G-transitivity. The formalization requires:

1. **Coset separation** (~80 lines): Given f ∈ S with f(q₀) ≠ 0, construct f' ∈ S supported only at q₀. Use `coset_chars_ne` (now proved) to find a ∈ A distinguishing two cosets, then apply (ρ(a,1) - β·id)/(α-β) to zero out one coset at a time. Induction on |support(f)|.

2. **Stabilizer acts on identity component** (~30 lines): Show H = G_χ preserves {f ∈ S | f supported at identity coset}, and this is a sub-H-representation of U. Note: the action at identity coset is U.ρ composed with conjugation by e.out (a Quotient.out artifact).

3. **Simplicity of U** (~10 lines): The identity component is a nonzero H-sub-rep of U. By `CategoryTheory.Simple U`, it equals U.

4. **G-transitivity** (~20 lines): For any coset q, pick g with g·e = q. Since g maps supported-at-e to supported-at-q, and S is G-invariant, S contains all of V_q for all q. Hence S = V.

5. **Bridge to categorical Simple** (~40 lines): Convert from "every nonzero invariant subspace is ⊤" to `CategoryTheory.Simple (inducedRepV φ χ U)`. Requires working with `Action.Hom`, `Mono`, `IsIso` in FDRep. No `NeZero (Nat.card G)` needed since we prove mono_isIso_iff_nonzero directly.

**Key blocker**: The bridge between `Submodule`-level irreducibility and categorical `Simple` for FDRep when the group is potentially infinite (no Maschke's theorem). The existing `FDRep.simple_of_isSimpleModule_asModule` requires `NeZero (Nat.card G : k)` which fails for infinite A ⋊ G.

### Proof strategies for (ii) and (iii)

Both require the same coset separation infrastructure as (i). Once (i) is proved:
- **(ii)** follows from: isomorphic reps have same A-character decomposition (same orbit), using `FDRep.char_iso` and the character formula.
- **(iii)** follows from: given simple W, restrict to A, find a character χ in W|_A, take irreducible G_χ-sub-rep U of the χ-weight space, construct V(χ,U) → W via Frobenius reciprocity, conclude iso by simplicity.

## Accomplished

Issue #1540: Proved `sl2_trivial_action_of_trivial_subquotients` in `Theorem2_1_1.lean`.

This lemma shows that if the Casimir element acts as zero on a finite-dimensional sl(2,C)-module, then sl(2) acts trivially (all brackets are zero).

Added 4 helper lemmas:
1. **`casimir_zero_of_submodule`** — Casimir restricts to zero on Lie submodules
2. **`casimir_zero_of_quotient`** — Casimir passes to zero on Lie quotients
3. **`sl2_trivial_of_dim_one`** — sl(2) acts trivially on 1-dimensional spaces (uses perfectness of sl(2))
4. **`sl2_trivial_of_casimir_zero`** — Strong induction on dimension: base cases (dim 0, irreducible with C=0 forces dim 1), inductive case (find proper nonzero Lie submodule, apply induction to it and the quotient, then use `sl2_acts_trivially_of_quotient_and_sub`)

Also added `import Mathlib.Algebra.Lie.Quotient` for `HasQuotient` on `LieSubmodule`.

Supersedes PR #1554 which had the same proof but couldn't merge due to conflicts with infrastructure commits #1542 and #1544.

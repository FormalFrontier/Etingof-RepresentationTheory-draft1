### Proved F⁻ functoriality: `reflectionFunctorMinus_map_iso` (1→0 sorry, #2065)

Constructed `Nonempty (QuiverRepresentation.Iso (F⁻ ρ₁) (F⁻ ρ₂))` given `σ : QuiverRepresentation.Iso ρ₁ ρ₂` in ReflectionFunctorInfrastructure.lean (+232 lines).

**Key technical challenge solved:** The v=i case of `equivAt` has TWO `@obj (reflectionFunctorMinus ...) v` references in its return type. Each contains a `Decidable.casesOn (inst i i)`. Lean's `rw` tactic computes a motive by abstracting over `inst i i` in the entire goal — with two casesOn terms, this causes exponential whnf blowup (timed out at 25.6M heartbeats).

**Solution:** `refine match inst i i with | .isTrue _ => ?_` resolves both casesOn via iota reduction instead of motive abstraction. This compiles within 1.6M heartbeats. The key insight is that `match` creates a dependent case split that simultaneously iota-reduces all `Decidable.casesOn` terms sharing the same discriminant, while `rw` must abstract over the discriminant position(s) which requires traversing and rebuilding the entire goal type.

- `directSumMapEquiv_lof`: compatibility with `DirectSum.lof`

**Naturality proof structure:**
- a=i: vacuous (i is a sink in the reversed quiver)
- a≠i, b≠i: reduces to σ.naturality via `mapLinear_ne_ne` + `LinearEquiv.symm_apply_eq`
- a≠i, b=i: reduces Quotient.equiv via `mapQ_apply` + `directSumMapEquiv_lof`

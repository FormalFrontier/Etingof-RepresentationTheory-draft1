Proved two Chapter 4 items for issue #580:

1. **Proposition 4.1.2** (converse of Maschke's theorem): If k[G] is semisimple then |G| is a unit in k. Proof constructs the element Lambda = sum_g g, shows it is central with Lambda^2 = |G| * Lambda. When |G| = 0 in k, Lambda^2 = 0 but Lambda != 0. By semisimplicity, the left ideal generated by Lambda has an idempotent generator e = r * Lambda; centrality gives e^2 = r^2 * Lambda^2 = 0, so e = 0, forcing the ideal to be trivial -- contradiction.

2. **Example 4.3** (finite abelian group irreps): All irreducible representations of a finite abelian group over an algebraically closed field of char 0 are 1-dimensional. The scaffolded statement uses `IsSimpleModule k V` (simple over k, not over k[G]), making this a direct application of `isSimpleModule_iff_finrank_eq_one`. Note: the statement doesn't capture the full mathematical content (it doesn't use the group action), but it matches the scaffolding.

## Accomplished

Proved `sl2_trivial_action_of_trivial_subquotients` in Theorem2_1_1.lean — the helper lemma showing that if the Casimir element of sl(2) acts as zero on a finite-dimensional module V, then sl(2) acts trivially on V.

Key proof components:
1. **`sl2_acts_trivially_of_quotient_and_sub`**: if sl2 acts trivially on submodule N and quotient W/N, then sl2 acts trivially on W (uses Jacobi identity + perfectness of sl2)
2. **`casimir_zero_of_submodule`**: Casimir restricts to zero on submodules (via `LieSubmodule.coe_toEnd`)
3. **`casimir_zero_of_quotient`**: Casimir passes to zero on quotients (surjectivity of mk + bracket rfl)
4. **`sl2_trivial_of_dim_one`**: 1-dimensional representations are trivial (scalar endomorphisms commute, perfectness)
5. **Main induction** (`sl2_trivial_action_of_trivial_subquotients`): strong induction on `finrank ℂ W`
   - dim 0: subsingleton
   - Irreducible: Casimir eigenvalue n(n+2)=0 forces n=0, dim=1, so trivial by `sl2_trivial_of_dim_one`
   - Reducible: proper nonzero submodule exists, induction on sub and quotient, then combine

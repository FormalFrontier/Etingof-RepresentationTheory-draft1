## Current frontier

The `indecomposable_reduces_to_simpleRoot` sorry remains. The proof requires establishing B(d(V), d(V)) = 2 for indecomposable V, which the existing `Theorem6_8_1` assumes rather than derives. The book's proof uses type-changing representation iteration (each F+ changes the quiver type), which is extremely complex to formalize.

Two viable approaches for future sessions:
1. **Type-changing iteration**: Formalize the reflection functor iteration at the representation level, threading Module.Free/Finite instances. Very complex but follows the book directly.
2. **Independent B(d,d) = 2**: Prove via homological algebra (dim End(V) = 1, Ext^1(V,V) = 0 for Dynkin indecomposables → Euler form = 1 → B = 2). Requires Ext infrastructure not currently available.

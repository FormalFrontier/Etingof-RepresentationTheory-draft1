Significant structural progress on `exists_orthogonal_idempotents_for_simples` (the last sorry in Theorem 9.2.1(i)):

1. **Proved quotient action factoring**: Elements in the same A/J-coset act identically on simple modules (`hsmul_eq`), and `smulRange` depends only on the A/J-image (`hsmulRange_eq`).

2. **Proved idempotent lifting**: Orthogonal idempotents in A/J lift to A via `OrthogonalIdempotents.lift_of_isNilpotent_ker`, with nilpotent kernel from semiprimary ring structure.

3. **Proved rank transfer**: The rank property (finrank of smulRange = δ_{ij}) transfers from A/J-idempotents to their lifts in A, since the action factors through the quotient.

4. **Proved orthogonality construction**: Given an injective block correspondence σ : ι → Fin n, the family `WA⁻¹(Pi.single (σ i) (Matrix.single 0 0 1))` forms orthogonal idempotents in A/J, using:
   - `matrix_single_zero_isIdempotentElem`: E₁₁ is idempotent
   - `orthogonalIdempotents_pi_single`: Pi.single of idempotents gives orthogonal family
   - `OrthogonalIdempotents.embedding` + `OrthogonalIdempotents.map`: transport through embedding and ring homomorphism

5. **Added import**: `Mathlib.RingTheory.SimpleModule.IsAlgClosed` for the algebraically closed Wedderburn-Artin theorem.

**Remaining sorry**: Reduced from the entire lemma to a single well-defined statement:
```
∃ (σ : ι → Fin n), Function.Injective σ ∧ ∀ i j (a : A), π a = WA⁻¹(Pi.single (σ i) E₁₁) → finrank k (smulRange M_j a) = δ_{ij}
```
This is the block-module correspondence: matching each simple module to its Wedderburn-Artin block and proving the rank-1 property.

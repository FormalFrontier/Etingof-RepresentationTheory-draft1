### Analysis: Why Both Directions Are Hard

Both directions require proving algebraic identities for operators T_i(f) in the non-commutative ring End(V^⊗n). The T_i pairwise commute (proved), but Mathlib's MvPolynomial APIs (including Newton's identities `MvPolynomial.mul_esymm_eq_sum`) require `CommSemiring` targets. Key blockers:

1. **⊆ direction:** Needs multilinear expansion of PiTensorProduct.map applied to sums (similar to the existing polarization_eq proof), plus Vandermonde matrix invertibility (available as `Matrix.det_vandermonde`), plus Lagrange interpolation coefficient extraction.

2. **⊇ direction:** Needs Newton's identity for pairwise-commuting elements in a non-commutative ring. Options: (a) prove Newton's identity from scratch via a combinatorial argument (50+ lines), or (b) construct a ring homomorphism from MvPolynomial to a commutative subalgebra.

Either direction is estimated at 50-80 lines of proof.

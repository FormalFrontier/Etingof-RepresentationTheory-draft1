## Analysis of proof difficulty

The column identity `∑_u w(u,c) = HP(μ)/HP(μ\c)` is genuinely hard. Attempted approaches:

1. **Direct algebraic manipulation**: The per-cell hook walk weights do NOT decompose as simple hook ratios (verified on 2×2 example). The sum is non-trivial.

2. **Induction on |μ|**: Requires relating hook products of μ\c₀\c to μ\c when removing two corners. The hook lengths change in both removals making the factorization messy.

3. **Double counting / Fubini**: Swapping sums gives `S = 1 + ∑_v f(v)×w(v,c)` where f(v) counts co-hook appearances. But f(v) has no clean closed form.

4. **Circular bootstrap**: Tried to derive the hook quotient identity from the IH + branching rule, but this is circular — the HQI is needed to prove the HLF, and the derivation requires the HLF.

5. **Telescoping product**: Only works for rectangular diagrams where colLen is constant along rows.

**Recommendation**: This may need a dedicated session focused on a single proof strategy carried through to completion, possibly following the original GNW paper's argument more closely, or using the Novelli-Pak-Stoyanovskii bijective approach.
